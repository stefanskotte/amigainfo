Amiga 500 / 500+

10 screws
QtySizeTypePlacement
3 M3 × 6 mm Phillips, convex head Case bottom — front edge Underside, along the front lip of the case
3 M3 × 8 mm Phillips, convex head Case bottom — rear edge Underside, along the back of the case
4 M3 × 6 mm Phillips Motherboard mounting 2× at front, 2× at left side
The metal RF shielding is held by 4 Phillips screws plus bent metal clips. The floppy drive uses 4 separate screws (1 side + 3 from bottom) with a different thread — do not mix these with case screws. Some early A500 units used Torx T9 or hex-socket (Allen) screws externally instead of Phillips.
Case bump warning: Using 8 mm screws in the front holes risks creating raised, discoloured marks on the top shell from over-tightening. Always use 6 mm screws for the front.

Amiga 600

6 screws
QtySizeTypePlacement
6 M3 × 6 mm Phillips Case bottom — all six positions All visible when flipped over; no screws behind the Commodore sticker
The A600 case also uses two side clips — one on the left near the floppy slot and one on the right near the PCMCIA port — that must be released with a flat screwdriver after removing the screws. The motherboard is not separately screwed down; it rests on standoffs held in place by the case halves. The floppy drive and hard disk caddy have their own separate mounting hardware.

Amiga 1200

10 screws
QtySizeTypePlacement
3 M3 × 6 mm Phillips, convex head Case bottom — front edge Underside, along front lip
2 M3 × 8 mm Phillips Case bottom — rear edge Above the mouse and joystick ports
2 M3 × 8 mm Phillips Case sides 1× left side by floppy drive, 1× right side by PCMCIA slot
3 M3 × 6 mm Phillips Motherboard mounting 1× front, 2× right side — varies by PCB revision
Rev 1D.4 motherboards lack the rightmost PCB section (mouse port area), so the floppy drive bracket and one motherboard screw position shift slightly left compared to earlier revisions. The bottom-left motherboard screw may use a metal flap or nut underneath. The internal floppy drive is secured by 2 separate bolts (M3 × 8 mm) through the rear of the case above the joystick/mouse ports.
Case bump warning: As with the A500, never use 8 mm screws in the front case holes — 6 mm only to prevent damage to the top shell.

Amiga 2000

~20+ screws
QtySizeTypePlacement
5 M4 × 5 mm Phillips, with locking washer External case (top cover) 2× left side, 2× right side, 1× rear
7 M4 × 5 mm Phillips Internal metal frame to lower case 3× front, 4× rear — holds drive cage and PSU frame
~6 M3 × 5 mm Phillips Motherboard to lower case Also used for CPU/video blanking panel screws
varies M3.5 × 5 mm Phillips, with locking washer Expansion card brackets One per installed Zorro II / ISA card bracket
2 M3 × 5 mm Phillips Power supply to internal frame Secures PSU inside the drive cage frame
The A2000 is a metal "big box" desktop — very different from the wedge models. All screws are standard Phillips machine screws (not self-tapping). Do not substitute PC-compatible screws — they have a different thread pitch and will strip the original threads. The expansion slot covers at the rear slightly overlap the motherboard's port shield; you must loosen the bottom screw of each cover and swing them aside before removing the motherboard.

Amiga 3000

~20+ screws

4–5 M4 × 5 mm Phillips, with nylon/vinyl washer External case (top cover) Same screw as A2000 — confirmed interchangeable
~7 M4 Phillips Internal frame (middle plate) to lower case Holds the drive cage, PSU, and mid-plate assembly
~6 M3 × 5 mm Phillips Motherboard mounting Secures main board to bottom case

The A3000 shares the same external M4 case screws as the A2000 (confirmed to be interchangeable). The original screws have colour-matched heads with a nylon washer — custom-made fasteners that are hard to source. The A3000's video slot bracket hole is smaller than the A2000's (standard PC/Zorro size), so A2000 video cards with oversized flat brackets will not physically line up. A3000D (desktop) and A3000T (tower) variants have different case layouts but share the same screw threading.

Common specifications across all models

The wedge models (A500, A600, A1200) all use M3 Phillips screws in 6 mm and 8 mm lengths. The big box models (A2000, A3000, A4000) use larger M4 screws for the metal case and M3/M3.5 for motherboard and card brackets. All are Phillips head throughout.

  • Wedge case M3 thread — 6 mm (front/motherboard) and 8 mm (rear/sides)
  • Big box case M4 × 5 mm with locking or nylon washer
  • Card brackets M3.5 × 5 mm with locking washer (A2000/A3000)
  • Motherboard M3 × 5 mm (big box) or M3 × 6 mm (wedge)
  • Head type Phillips throughout — #2 driver for most screws
  • Material Steel (originals) or stainless steel (replacements)
  • Key rule Do not use PC-compatible screws — different thread pitch will damage threads
